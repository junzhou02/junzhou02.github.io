I am an M.E. student at Huazhong University of Science and Technology, working at the intersection of dynamical systems, control, stochastic modeling, and Koopman-inspired analysis.

My research is shaped by training across mathematics and systems engineering, and by time spent at Manchester, Waterloo, and Toronto. I am especially interested in how operator-theoretic and optimization-based ideas can help us understand complex systems that are nonlinear, noisy, or infinite-dimensional.

Current interests: PDE control, stochastic system identification, spectral representations, and learning meaningful structure from data.

About

I started in mathematics, where I was drawn to approximation, geometry, and the internal logic of formal systems. Over time, my attention shifted toward systems that evolve in time and resist neat closed-form descriptions. That shift led me into control, stochastic dynamics, and data-driven operator methods.

Across my recent work, I keep returning to the same question: how can we build models that are not only predictive, but also reveal the structure of a system well enough to reason about stability, control, and long-term behavior?

Now

  • I am currently studying feedback optimization for infinite-dimensional linear systems, with attention to Lyapunov-based guarantees and real-time regulation.
  • I am also working on identification of stochastic dynamical systems through Yosida-style approximations of Koopman generators.
  • More broadly, I enjoy research problems where analysis, data, and dynamical structure need to speak to each other.
